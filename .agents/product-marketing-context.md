## Product Overview
**One-liner:** A small product studio that builds websites, web apps, mobile apps, e-commerce, AI tools, and automation — end to end, no handoffs.

**What it does:** Techyor is a digital product studio offering strategy, design, and development under one roof. One team takes ideas from discovery through to shipped product, covering everything from landing pages to AI tools to mobile apps. Engagements range from $200 5-page sites to multi-month custom builds.

**Product category:** Digital product agency / product studio (how customers search: "web development agency", "custom software development", "mobile app development", "AI development agency", "hire React/Node/Python developers").

**Product type:** Services / agency. Productized at the entry level ($200 5-pager) and bespoke at the upper end.

**Business model:**
- Project-based pricing (fixed-scope) **from $200 (basic 5-page site) up to ~$20K (custom builds, apps, AI tools)**
- Dedicated full-time hires (embedded senior developers)
- Part-time / fractional (2–3 days/week)
- Fixed-scope projects
- Monthly post-launch support plans (after the included 30 days)

**Team:** Small, fully-built studio. India-based, English-fluent, working in client time zones. Founders/leads include Rishab Singla and Munish.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Building software is expensive, slow, and risky. Most agencies overcharge for generic work, miss deadlines, or hand off between strategy → design → dev teams that don't talk to each other.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Big agencies: expensive, slow, dilute senior talent across many accounts
- Freelance marketplaces: cheap but inconsistent, no strategy layer, you become PM
- In-house hires: 3–6 month ramp, hard to find senior talent, expensive
- AI-only "build it yourself" tools: brittle output, no design taste, no strategy

**What it costs them:**
- Lost launch windows / runway burn
- Rebuilds 18 months later when bad code can't scale
- Generic design that doesn't convert
- Founder time consumed managing vendors

**Emotional tension:** Fear of being burned again by an agency. Fear of picking the wrong tech stack. Pressure to ship before runway runs out.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct (other product studios):** WIP / Significa / Thoughtbot / Hanno / Ueno-style boutique studios — falls short because they're either pricier, slower, or focused on enterprise.

**Direct (offshore agencies):** Generic India/Eastern European dev shops — falls short on design taste, strategy depth, and communication quality.

**Secondary (freelance marketplaces):** Upwork/Toptal direct hires — falls short because client has to assemble the team, manage strategy, and own QA.

**Secondary (productized agencies):** Designjoy / Bezel / monthly subscription studios — falls short for anything beyond simple design work.

**Indirect (AI builders):** Lovable / v0 / Bolt / Cursor — falls short when the project needs real backend, custom design, or production-grade code.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- **End-to-end under one roof** — strategy, design, dev. No handoffs.
- **$200 entry point** — uncommon for studios offering this level of craft
- **Daily updates** — not weekly, not "we'll circle back"
- **Senior-only team** — no juniors hidden behind the pitch deck
- **Code you own, NDA-friendly** — clean handoff, nothing recycled
- **30 days post-launch support included**
- **8+ years, 80+ products shipped** — track record
- **Upwork-verified track record** — visible 5-star reviews, repeat clients

**How we do it differently:** Strategy first. Design second. Code built to last. One small team owns the whole arc — so context never gets lost between phases.

**Why that's better:** Faster iteration, less rework, higher craft, and the person who designed it also defends it in code review.

**Why customers choose us:** Premium craft at offshore-friendly pricing, with senior-level communication and a track record they can verify.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Current agency missed deadlines, ghosted, or shipped generic work. Freelancer flaked. In-house hire taking too long to find.

**Pull:** Visible portfolio, verified reviews, fast response, senior team on the call, clear pricing, daily-update promise.

**Habit:** Sticking with an existing dev shop "because we already started." Defaulting to Upwork posting and screening 40 applicants.

**Anxiety:** "What if they're cheaper because they're worse?" "What if they disappear mid-project?" "Will I own the code?" "How will time zones work?"
